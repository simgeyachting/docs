## How Visa-Free Travel Works

**You can visit the Greek Islands without a Schengen visa.** Under the "visa-free island visit" policy in effect since 2018:

- ✅ Only a valid passport is required
- ✅ Maximum stay of 7 days
- ✅ Island access only (not Athens or mainland)
- ✅ Valid for Turkish citizens

### Required Documents

1. **Passport** (valid for at least 6 months)
2. **Ferry/Boat ticket** (round trip)
3. **Accommodation booking** (not required for day trips)

## Kos Island Tour

### Why Kos?

**The closest and most popular option.** Only 45 minutes from Bodrum.

<Card
  title="Distance to Kos"
  icon="route"
  body="12 nautical miles from Bodrum — 45 minutes by fast boat"

### What to See in Kos

**Hippocrates Tree** — 2,500-year-old legendary tree where Hippocrates taught

**Asklepion** — Ancient medical center, Hippocrates' medical school

**Kos Castle** — Built by the Knights of St. John, commanding harbor views

**Plaka Beach** — Turquoise waters, sunbed and umbrella service

## Kalimnos Tour

### Why Kalimnos?

**The island of sponge divers.** Less touristy, more authentic.

<Card
  title="Kalimnos Feature"
  icon="star"
  body="World-famous sponge production — still handmade"
/>

### What to Do in Kalimnos

- **Sponge Museum** — Discover the island's history
- **Pothia Town** — Colorful neoclassical houses
- **Telendos Island** — Small island opposite, 10 min by boat
- **Swimming coves** — Crystal clear waters
