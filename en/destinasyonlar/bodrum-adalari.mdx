# Bodrum Islands: Hidden Paradises of the Aegean

<img src="/images/galeri/simge002.jpg" alt="Bodrum Islands" style={{borderRadius: "12px", marginBottom: "24px", width: "100%" }} />


The Bodrum Peninsula is a favorite not only for its unique coves but also for the enchanting islands surrounding it. During boat tours and blue voyages with Simge Yachting, you will have the opportunity to discover the unique beauty of each of these islands. Here are the main islands of Bodrum and its surroundings:

## 1. Karaada

One of the closest and most popular islands to Bodrum, Karaada is famous for its healing hot springs and mud baths. The mineral-rich mud from the island's cave is believed to be beneficial for the skin. Karaada is an essential stop on daily boat tours and is ideal for both swimming and relaxation.

## 2. Orak Island

Known as the Maldives of Bodrum, Orak Island resembles an aquarium with its turquoise clear waters and white sandy beach. Thanks to its rich underwater life, it is a perfect spot for snorkeling and diving. The calm atmosphere of the island is ideal for those seeking a peaceful day.

## 3. Catalada

Located west of Bodrum, Catalada takes its name from the combination of three small islets. It is a popular spot especially for diving enthusiasts. The underwater caves and rich marine life around the island offer an unforgettable diving experience. It is one of the calm coves where swimming breaks are taken during boat tours.

## 4. Tavsan Island (Gumusluk)

Accessible on foot from Gumusluk beach, Tavsan Island stands out with its historical ruins and natural beauty. Located on the ruins of the ancient city of Myndos, the island attracts photography enthusiasts especially with its sunset views. The area around the island is also suitable for swimming.

## 5. Little Orak Island

Located near Orak Island, Little Orak Island has a quieter and more pristine atmosphere. It is ideal for those who want to get away from the crowds and be alone with nature. You can swim in its clear waters and enjoy the peaceful environment of the island.
