# Tours from Bodrum to the Greek Islands: Discover Beyond the Aegean

<img src="/images/galeri/simge005.jpg" alt="Greek Islands" style={{borderRadius: "12px", marginBottom: "24px", width: "100%" }} />


Are you ready to explore the enchanting Greek Islands on the opposite shore of the Aegean Sea, departing from Bodrum with Simge Yachting? With their historical textures, unique beaches, authentic villages, and lively atmospheres, the Greek Islands will add a different dimension to your blue voyage experience. Here are some popular Greek Islands you can easily reach from Bodrum and visit with our boat tours:

## 1. Kos Island

A captivating island jewel nestled in the deep blue embrace of the Aegean Sea, Kos stands out with its rich history, sunny beaches (Tigaki, Paradise Beach), and vibrant culture. You can explore historical sites like the Ancient Gymnasium and Asklepion, and enjoy pleasant time in the lively center of the island. Daily boat tours from Bodrum to Kos and return options from Kos to Bodrum are available.

## 2. Kalimnos Island

A fascinating island where rugged landscapes meet crystal waters and adventure calls at every opportunity. Kalimnos is a world-renowned center especially for rock climbing enthusiasts. With its pastoral beaches (Myrties, Arginonta) and traditional villages (Pothia, Vathy), it offers a calm and authentic Greek island experience.

## 3. Leros Island

An island notable for its calm landscapes, picturesque villages, and rich historical texture. Leros is ideal for those seeking a peaceful holiday away from the crowds. Its blue waters, secluded beaches (Alinda), historical windmills, and cultural heritage like Panteli Castle are among its attractions.

## 4. Patmos Island

A mystical island where timeless beauty combines with spiritual tranquility. Patmos is an important pilgrimage center for Christianity, home to spiritual heritage sites such as the Cave of the Apocalypse where the Book of Revelation was written, and the Monastery of Saint John the Theologian. It is also known for its pastoral beaches like Psili Ammos and the lively Skala harbor.
