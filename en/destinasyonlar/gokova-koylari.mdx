# Gökova Coves: The Heart of the Blue Voyage

<img src="/images/galeri/07169a18-459e-4391-ae69-f7c699c5be63.jpeg" alt="Gokova Coves" style={{borderRadius: "12px", marginBottom: "24px", width: "100%" }} />


Located at the meeting point of the Aegean and Mediterranean, Gökova Gulf is one of the indispensable routes of the blue voyage with its natural beauty, crystal clear turquoise waters, and untouched coves. With Simge Yachting, you'll discover these unique paradises of Gökova and experience an unforgettable holiday surrounded by peace and nature.

Here are some popular coves and islands you must see in Gökova Gulf:

## 1. Sedir Island (Cleopatra Beach)

One of Gökova's most famous stops, Sedir Island is known for its legendary Cleopatra Beach. According to legend, the sand of this beach where Cleopatra and Antonius once swam has a special geological structure and it is forbidden to take it off the island. The island's historical ruins and unique nature are also worth seeing.

## 2. English Harbor

This natural harbor where British ships took shelter during World War II is known for its calm and sheltered structure. Surrounded by pine trees, English Harbor is an ideal place for swimming, relaxing, and enjoying nature. You can go snorkeling in its clear waters.

## 3. Orak Island

Orak Island, which we also mentioned among the Bodrum coves, is one of the popular stops on Gökova routes. It stands out with its Maldives-like turquoise sea and underwater richness. It is indispensable especially for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts.

## 4. Cokertme Cove

Known for its famous folk song, Cokertme is a charming fishing village with a calm cove. You can anchor here, taste local flavors, and relax in the peaceful atmosphere of the village. It is a peaceful stop with its surrounding greenery and clear sea.

## 5. Karacasogut Cove

Known for its proximity to Marmaris, Karacasogut is one of the important stops of Gökova blue voyages. You can go ashore here to explore the surroundings and take a break at the village restaurants. With its calm and sheltered structure, it is one of the safe harbors for boats.

## 6. Longoz Cove

Longoz Cove, where pine trees extend all the way to the sea, is mesmerizing with its natural beauty. With its calm and peaceful atmosphere, it is ideal for those who want to relax and be alone with nature. You can swim here and take short walks in the forest.
